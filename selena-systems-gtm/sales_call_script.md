# Sales Call System — Selena Systems (Villa Ops OS)

Goal: close $5,000–$10,000 fixed-scope implementations. Tone throughout: calm diagnostician, not pitcher. You are the specialist who measured their response gap; they should do 70% of the talking. Adapt bracketed lines for Backup Offers.

## 1. 15-minute qualification call

**Purpose:** decide whether a 45-minute discovery is worth both parties' time. Book it from the audit video or a walk-in.

**Opening (1 min):** "Thanks for the fifteen minutes, ⟨name⟩. My plan: five questions about how inquiries flow through ⟨Company⟩ today, I'll tell you honestly whether my system fits, and if it does we book a proper working session. If it doesn't fit, I'll say so and point you somewhere useful. Fair?"

**The five questions (10 min):**
1. "Walk me through what happens when a guest inquires at 9pm on a Saturday — WhatsApp, website, wherever. Who sees it, and when?"
2. "Roughly how many inquiries land in a normal week, across all channels?" *(Under ~20/week → pain may be too small; note it.)*
3. "Who answers them today — how many people, and is it their whole job?" *(Hiring or hired reservation staff = strong signal.)*
4. "When a booking is lost, do you find out? Where would you even look?" *(No pipeline = the wedge.)*
5. "If this were solved — every inquiry answered in two minutes, every lead visible — what would that be worth to the business? Ballpark is fine."

**Qualification gates (in your head):**
- **Entity/billing (Task Zero):** "One practical thing — for contracting, do you invoice through an Indonesian company or an overseas entity?" *(Indonesian entity only → park politely per legal advice; stay friends; ask for referrals.)*
- Volume ≥ ~20 inquiries/week, decision-maker on the call or one intro away, can start within 2 weeks, no procurement maze.

**Close of call (3 min):**
- Fits: "This is exactly the situation the system is built for. Next step is a 45-minute working session — I'll bring a demo answering *your* inquiries, we'll map your flow, and I'll leave you exact numbers and a fixed price. ⟨Day⟩ or ⟨day⟩?"
- Doesn't fit: "Honest answer: at your volume the math doesn't justify my fee. Here's what I'd do instead ⟨one genuine tip⟩. And if you know an operator drowning in WhatsApp, I'd value the introduction."

## 2. 45-minute discovery call

**Prep (before):** secret-shop results, demo agent loaded with 2–3 of their actual villas/listings, their reviews skimmed, ROI sheet open.

**Agenda (2 min):** "Three parts: I map how inquiries work here today — you talk, I draw. Then I show the system on your own villas. Then we do the math together and I give you a fixed price. Stop me anywhere."

**Part A — Diagnosis (20 min).** Map on shared screen/paper: every inquiry channel → who monitors → response SLA by hour-of-day → where it's recorded → follow-up? → booking → what falls through. Questions that surface cost:
- "What time zones do your guests mostly inquire from?" → overlay their coverage hours; the gap draws itself. Then volunteer the OTA boundary (red team R5): "One honest limit: Airbnb and Booking chat can't be integrated without a PMS — their APIs are closed. We cover WhatsApp, your site, Instagram, email, and we parse OTA notification emails into the pipeline. Your OTA apps keep doing what they already do." Volunteering this is a credibility win.
- "Show me where last Tuesday's inquiries live right now." *(The squirm here is the sale.)*
- "How many of the questions your team answered today were one of the same twenty questions?"
- "What happened the last time ⟨reservations person⟩ was sick or on leave?"
- "If I asked how many leads you got last month and how many booked, could anyone answer without guessing?"

**Part B — Demo (10 min).** Live: inquiry in English about real dates/rates → 2-min accurate answer; same in German/Bahasa; then a trap question ("can I get 40% off for 3 months?") → watch it escalate to a human with context, *not* answer. "That's the most important thing you'll see today: what it does when it doesn't know."

**Part C — The math + close (13 min).** ROI framework (§4) with their numbers, then: "Fixed scope, fixed price: ⟨$7,500 / founding $5,000⟩. $2,000 starts it; the balance is due at go-live — objectively defined, day 10–17 — after your team has watched it draft replies for a week. If it fails the measured day-7 gate, you stop and owe nothing more. I can start ⟨date⟩. Proposal in your inbox today — want it addressed to you or a partner too?"

## 3. Diagnosis framework (the mental model)

Score each 1–5 during discovery; 3+ areas at 4–5 = strong fit; anything at 5 = lead the pitch with it.
1. **Coverage gap** — hours/week inquiries arrive unmonitored (nights, weekends, staff leave)
2. **Channel scatter** — # of places a lead can arrive vs. # actually monitored; leads on personal phones
3. **Repetition load** — % of messages that are the same top-20 questions
4. **Pipeline blindness** — can they count last month's leads and their outcomes?
5. **Follow-up decay** — does anyone re-contact a guest who went quiet? Ever?
6. **Key-person risk** — does response capability live in one person's head/phone?

## 4. ROI calculation framework (do it WITH them, their numbers only)

```
I  = inquiries per month                       (their number)
L  = share arriving out-of-hours/answered >1h  (from secret shop + their estimate)
C  = current inquiry→booking conversion        (their number; if unknown — pipeline blindness, note it)
V  = average booking value                     (their number)
U  = uplift on slow-handled share if answered fast
     (use conservative 10–25%; NEVER promise — cite that 5-min responses
      convert dramatically better, source in market_research.md, then let
      THEM pick the uplift they believe)
Recovered revenue/month ≈ I × L × C × U × V
Compare against: system cost (one-time) + ~$50–150/month tools
             vs. current answer: reservation staff salary (recurring) that still sleeps at night
```
Say: "I'm not going to promise you revenue — anyone who does is guessing with your money. Let's use *your* numbers and *your* assumed uplift, and you tell me if the math clears the bar." *(A payback they computed themselves is unarguable.)*

## 5. Objection handling

**"You have no case studies."** → "Right — I'm early, and that's exactly what the founding price and shadow-mode guarantee compensate for. You watch it answer *your* inquiries for a week before the balance exists. You're not trusting my portfolio; you're trusting your own eyes. And in return for the discount I ask for the case study."

**"AI will embarrass us with guests."** → "That fear is correct for most bots. This one answers only from the knowledge base you approve, and its default when unsure is to hand off to your team, not improvise — contractually. Plus seven days of shadow mode where it can't send anything. What would you need to see in those drafts to be comfortable?"

**"Too expensive."** → "Compared to what alternative? Doing nothing costs the missed bookings we just calculated — ⟨their number⟩ a month. Hiring covers eight hours a day, recurs forever, and churns. If the math we did together doesn't clear the bar with your own assumptions, tell me — then I'm genuinely the wrong purchase."

**"We'll do it ourselves / my nephew knows AI."** → "You probably could — the tools are public. What you're buying is that it's live in 14 days, that someone owns the edge cases when a guest asks something weird in German at 3am, and that it's my problem until it works. DIY projects like this usually die at 'mostly working.' If you have someone who'll own it end to end, honestly, use them."

**"Now's not a good time / after high season."** → "That's like fixing the roof after the rain. High season is when the leaky-lead cost peaks — every unanswered night is your most expensive night. Shadow mode means zero disruption to your team mid-season. What specifically would need to be true for the timing to work?"

**"Send me something / I'll think about it."** → "Of course. So I send something useful rather than a brochure: what's the main open question — the price, whether it'll work on your setup, or getting ⟨partner⟩ on board?" *(Then handle THAT.)*

**"We tried a chatbot; it was useless."** → "So has half the island — self-configured bots plateau fast; that's documented. The difference here isn't the bot, it's the implementation: your knowledge base, your escalation rules, tested on your real inquiries in shadow mode before it goes near a guest."

## 6. Closing script

"Let me summarize what we found: ⟨N⟩ inquiries a month, ⟨X hours⟩ typical after-hours wait, no single pipeline, and the math you just did says roughly ⟨$Y⟩/month walking away. The fix is fixed-scope: live in 14 days at ⟨price⟩, $2,000 to start, balance at go-live, and the measured day-7 kill switch if the drafts don't convince you. The only real decision today is whether we start ⟨date A⟩ or ⟨date B⟩ — which works?"

*(Then be quiet. If yes:)* "Good decision. Proposal and starter invoice today; kickoff workshop is two hours — bring whoever knows the villas best. Welcome aboard."

## 9. Payment terms — recommendation

**Use: $2,000 fixed starter + balance at objectively-defined go-live (day 10–17), for the first ~3 (founding) clients.** Then move to **50% / 50%**. (Starter raised from $1,500 per red team R13 — it must cover the real cost of days 0–7, since a stopping client keeps their KB content.)

Why not 100% upfront: with zero case studies, a $7,500 upfront wire to a new solo vendor is the single hardest ask in the whole funnel (Judge 3: it "fails first contact"); the trust ladder converts strictly better and the shadow-mode gate caps Selena's downside to well-paid discovery work. Why not 50/50 immediately: $3,750 upfront is still a big blind bet on a no-proof vendor; $2,000 stays near the "meeting-worthy risk" threshold for this buyer and gets more starts, which is what the case-study engine needs most right now. Once 2–3 case studies exist, 50/50 (then 100% with a discount as an *option*) both derisks Selena's calendar and signals scarcity honestly. Always: invoice in USD (or AED for Dubai) to offshore entities; never net-30 in Indonesia (45% of B2B invoices there run overdue — sourced in `market_research.md`).
