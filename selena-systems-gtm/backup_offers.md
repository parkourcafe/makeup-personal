# Backup Offers — Selena Systems

Two runner-up offers from the tournament (`idea_tournament.md`). Both share most of their build and sales motion with the winning Villa Ops OS, which is deliberate: if the winner stalls, the pivot costs days, not weeks. Backup 1 runs on the *same streets* as the winner; Backup 2 is the *scale market* the winner's case study unlocks — and it becomes the PRIMARY offer if Task Zero (entity screening, `winning_offer.md` §17) fails.

---

## Backup 1 — Bali Buyer Concierge™ (tournament aggregate: 190/300)

**AI lead qualification & follow-up for Bali real estate agencies selling to foreign buyers.**

- **Target:** Bali agencies/developers selling villas & land to international buyers; owner or sales director; 16 leads in `lead_list.csv` (screen for offshore billing entities first — same Task Zero).
- **Pain (documented):** slow peak-season responses called out in public reviews (Propertia), ignored emails (TripAdvisor threads), 1,500+ listing portfolios on small teams (Kibarer), hiring posts for manual inquiry-conversion roles (Betterplace). Industry-wide: 48% of broker inquiries never answered, 917-min average response ([WAV Group](https://www.wavgroup.com/2014/01/13/agent-responsiveness-study-reveals-critical-flaws-in-real-estate-lead-response/)).
- **Promise:** in 14 days, every buyer inquiry is qualified (budget, timeline, leasehold/freehold intent) within 2 minutes and nurtured until viewing — with full pipeline visibility.
- **Deliverables:** AI responder on WhatsApp + web forms; qualification flows; listing answers from inventory sheet (strict no-hallucination guardrail — the agent NEVER answers legal/ownership-structure questions; those route to a human with a disclaimer, since a bot misstating leasehold law to a $299k buyer is the deal-killer risk the skeptic flagged); viewing scheduler; long-cycle nurture sequences; CRM pipeline; training + docs.
- **Price:** $7,000–$9,000 fixed; founding-client $5,000 with case-study clause. Same trust-ladder payments as the winner ($1,500 start → balance at go-live; shadow-mode guarantee).
- **ROI logic:** median foreign transaction ~$299k ([market data](https://investlandbali.com/bali-real-estate-market)); commission ~5% is an ASSUMPTION to verify per agency — at anything near it, one incremental deal ≈ $15k, ~2x the fee. Sell on response-time and pipeline metrics (provable in weeks), not closed deals (provable in months) — this answers the skeptic's attribution attack.
- **When to activate:** run alongside the winner from day 1 — it's the same geographic circuit; pitch whichever pain the office you're standing in has. Promote to primary if villa PMs stall in week 1–2.
- **Key risks:** same legal gate; commission assumption unverified; long buyer cycles delay revenue-based proof (mitigated by selling operational metrics).

## Backup 2 — Speed-to-Lead OS™ for Dubai Brokerages (tournament aggregate: 180/300)

**AI lead response, qualification and 90-day follow-up for small/mid Dubai real estate brokerages.**

- **Target:** Dubai brokerages, 5–50 agents, buying Bayut/Property Finder leads; owner/managing director; 16 leads in `lead_list.csv`.
- **Pain (documented):** leads cost AED 300–600 and the same lead hits 3–4 agencies ([benchmarks](https://fwddigi.com/ae/blogs/cost-per-lead-dubai-benchmarks/), [groovyweb](https://www.groovyweb.co/blog/dubai-real-estate-lead-management)); 90%+ of communication on WhatsApp with follow-up trapped on agents' personal phones ([sudonum](https://www.sudonum.com/blog/the-rise-of-whatsapp-in-dubais-real-estate-market-a-sudonum-perspective)); brokerages hire "Lead Coordinators" for repetitive follow-up ([job post](https://www.die-geissens-realestate.com/careers/lead-coordinator-dubai/en)); AED 13.59bn commissions across 32,294 brokers in 2025 ([Arabian Business](https://www.arabianbusiness.com/real-estate/dubai-real-estate-broker-commissions-hit-3-7bn-as-number-of-brokers-reaches-32294)).
- **Promise:** in 14 days, every portal/WhatsApp lead gets an intelligent response in under 60 seconds, is qualified, logged, and followed up automatically for 90 days.
- **Deliverables:** AI WhatsApp responder + qualifier (budget/area/timeline); portal-lead ingestion (scoped precisely at audit — "whatever CRM they run" is a scope grenade, so the fixed price covers ONE named CRM + ONE portal source, extras quoted); hot-lead alerts; 90-day nurture; management dashboard (which also answers the agent-adoption risk: managers finally see response times per lead — sell it to the owner as visibility, position it to agents as "the bot does your boring follow-up").
- **Price:** $8,000–$10,000 fixed; founding-client $6,500. Trust ladder is mandatory here (cold + remote): free 3-min video audit → live demo on a recorded sample lead → $1,500 paid pilot on ONE agent's lead flow for 2 weeks → full rollout balance.
- **ROI logic:** one median-deal commission ≈ AED 40,000 (~$10,900) — one saved lead repays everything; their own marketing spend (AED 15–40k/month) anchors the fee as ~1–2 weeks of ad budget.
- **When to activate:** async from day 1 (video audits + complaint mining while working Bali in person); expect first close weeks 4–8 per the judges — faster with the Bali case study, immediately primary if Task Zero kills Bali-local selling.
- **Key risks:** cold-remote trust (mitigated by the pilot ladder), agent adoption sabotage (mitigated by owner-dashboard framing), integration sprawl (mitigated by scoped fixed price), the WAV Group stat is US/2014 — use the Dubai-specific sources above in-pitch instead.
