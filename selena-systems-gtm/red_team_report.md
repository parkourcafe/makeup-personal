# Red Team Report — Selena Systems GTM Package

A dedicated red-team agent attacked the complete package (all deliverables + datasets + verification results) across market choice, pricing, closeability, lead quality, outreach, delivery, trust/proof, legal/compliance, competition, founder fit, and cross-document consistency. The full unabridged attack is preserved at `research/raw/red-team-draft.md`. This report lists every issue with its severity, the fix, and the fix's status — **all fixes marked APPLIED are already incorporated into the final package.**

Separately, two verification agents spot-checked the datasets: pain signals 20/20 corroborated at exact URLs (0 suspect); leads 24 sampled → 21 verified, 1 corroborated, 2 field errors (both corrected). Details: `source_log.md`.

## What the red team said the package gets right

Honesty infrastructure (assumption log, ASSUMPTION labels, self-reporting verification); a tournament whose skeptics landed real blows and whose judge modifications were actually propagated; the secret-shop → 3-minute audit → shadow-mode-guarantee sales machinery ("a genuinely strong zero-proof opener"); delivery realism (shadow→supervised→autonomous ladder, anti-hallucination guardrails, prompt-injection tests, client-owned accounts, fail-safe to staff); differentiated positioning (published fixed pricing, no-PMS wedge, founding-client trade stated openly); and region-appropriate payment hygiene.

## Residual risks the founder must own (cannot be fixed in documents)

1. **The legal consult itself** (R1/R11) — booked and answered before any Bali-local selling; entity confirmed before any invoicing.
2. **Lawyer pass on the proposal's legal annex + PI insurance** (R10) — once, before the first signature.
3. **Price-ladder risk** (R12) — only real sales data will show whether $7,500 holds after founding clients.
4. **Platform dependency** (R4) — WhatsApp policy can tighten further; architecture mitigates, doesn't eliminate.
5. **Verification ceiling** (source_log) — the research environment couldn't direct-fetch some sources; re-verify any stat before quoting it in a live sales conversation.
